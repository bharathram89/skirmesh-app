SkirMesh is poised to revolutionize the game of Airsoft through mesh networking and RFID technology. Uniquely leveraging desired attributes of each, the airsoft field becomes a dynamic and interactive experience on and off the field.

The node

The node is the foundation of the system and permits player interaction. Dozens can populate the field and take on different functions to allow the battlefield architect to design complex, dynamic gameplay scenarios. More nodes means higher fidelity control over every underlying gameplay mechanic.

Interacations are made possible with RFID tags or wristbands. Each RFID tag possesses a Unique Identifier (UID) to uniquely identify who interacted with the node. Information gathered by the node is passed to the controller and certain interactions (such as captures or assists) may be awarded points.

node Interaction

Interaction with the node is simple. Each issued RFID tag or wristband, when presented within 1 inch of the area marked with glow-in-the-dark tape will be acknowledged by the node. Acknowledgement may be in the form of flashing lights, chirping sounds, or both.

node Configurations

nodes can be configured to serve different purposes based on the game play requirements. These include:

  • Control Points
  • Respawn Stations
  • Bombs / IEDs
  • Team Registration
  • Team Query
  • UID Registration

Control Point

Control point is the core function of the node. We set out to remove confusion and disputes that often occur in control point style games. This function allows also areas of the airsoft field to be directly mapped to the node. If your team doesn't own the node for that territory then your team doesn't own that territory.

The node displays the assigned color of the team that owns it with its 5 RGB LEDs. Additionally, you can checkout the field map and see the color of the territory for the node in question to determine the owner.

Capturing a Control Point

Here is a scenario on how to capture a Control Point. Lets say you are assigned to RED team, you and your squad approaches a node. It displays 5 BLUE RGBs (it is owned by BLUE team). In order to take it over swipe your RFID tag over the designated area. You will hear short "beep" and then one of 2 things will happen based on the setting for the control points for the game:

  • Scenario One: If the control points have been configured for immediate capture the lights will all immediately go RED indicating your team now owns the node and associated territory.

  • Scenario Two: Control Points have been configured for a delayed capture. In this scenario all RGBs will start to blink in the color of the original owning team. As time towards the delayed capture counts down the RGBs will start to turn solid RED, which represents your progress toward completing capture. Each RGB indicates 1/5 of the total capture time, so dig in - your mission isn't accomplished until all RGBs are the color of your team.

For scenario 2, the entire time the RGBs are blink, the node is considered unstable. If BLUE team is able to eliminate your squad, they are able to recapture the node before RED team is awarded the capture. However, they must hold the Control Point for the amount of time RED had it in their control. For example, capture time is set for 60 seconds. RED begins the capture and holds it for 36 seconds (The node will display 3 RED RGBs and 2 BLUE RGBs, 36/60 = 3/5). BLUE eliminates all RED players and scans one of their RFID tags, BLUE will now how to hold that control point for 36 seconds before it becomes stable and fully in BLUE teams possession.

To reward players for working as a team we have included a capture assist function. Based on the value of this setting it allows multiple members of the same time to contribute toward the capture of a control point. Once capture of a control point has started, each subsequent team member that swipes their RFID tag on the unstable node will reduce the total capture by a fraction of the total capture time.

Example: Capture time = 60 seconds, Capture Assist = 50%. First player initiates capture, timer starts. 10 seconds into the capture another player from the same team swipes their RFID tag on the unstable node. Capture time has now been reduced by 30 seconds resulting in a total time remaining of 20 seconds (60 - 10 - 30 = 20)

This same behavior also works for a team trying to recapture a control point

Respawn Stations

Respawn Stations are really just an extension of the control point configuration. Based on game requirements certain control points can also serve as respawn stations, which allows certain control points to posses greater tactical value to team commanders as players will not have to travel to a respawn spawn station on the other side of the field.

Here is how the respawn mechanic works. After being elminated, you would return to the nearest control point that also serves as a respawn station. You will then hold your RFID tag over the node until all RGBs flash red. By doing that you have just deactivated your RFID tag from interacting with any other non respawn station nodes and the Controller is currently tracking you as "dead". THIS IS IMPORTANT PLEASE KEEP READING! In order to get your tag reactivated you need to wait for the cool down timer timer to elapse. You can check the status of this timer by holding your RFID tag over the node to see how many red lights appear. Again each red RGB indicates 1/5 the total time toward the cool down being completed. Only when all 5 RGBs flash green is your RFID tag re-activated and able to interact with other nodes on the field.

Bombs / IEDs

When configured as a Bomb/IED players are able to arm or disarm them by swiping their RFID tag. The arming, fuse, and difusing times are all configurable by the controller.

Arming

To arm the bomb, the player would hold their RFID band over the node for the duration of time set by the arming parameter. As the player holds their RFID band over the node, the RGBs will start to turn orange again following the 1/5 rule explained above. Once all RBGs turn orange they will immediately all turn red and flash periodiclly to indicate the bomb is armed. The red RGBs will turn on 1 by 1 based on total time remaining. Onces all RGBs are turned on, the bomb will "EXPLODE!" The node will flash all 5 RGBs red and the siren will give off a continuous and extremely annoying tone. Power cycling the node is the only way to reset the bomb.

Disarming

Disarming follows the same method as above. However, the node does not give feedback on the amount of of time is left to disarming is complete. Knowing what the disarm time is and looking at the lights remaining is the only way of knowing if you will be successful in disarming or become collateral damage.

Team Registration

Airsoft is a team sport and the Team Registration functionality allows the SkirMesh System to assign players and their associated UID to a team. The to identify a node that is in the team register mode it will have 2 RGBs illuminated the color of the team that they will assign the player to.

To assign yourself to a team, find the node that displays your team color and swipe your RFID tag. The node will provide a short beep indicating successful assignment to that team. Thats it!

Keep in mind, all SkirMesh game modes require a player to be part of a team. If you are unsure if your RFID tag is associated with a team either re-register or find a Team Query node(explained below).

Team Query

A team query node has just the single white LED illuminated. When you swipe your RFID tag, it will display your team color on the first 2 RGBs and the last RGB will be green if you are being tracked as "alive" or red if "dead."
